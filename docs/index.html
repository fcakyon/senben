Abstract

Content moderation systems classify images as safe or unsafe but lack spatial grounding and interpretability: they cannot explain what sensitive behavior was detected, who is involved, or where it occurs. We introduce the Sensitive Benchmark (SenBen), the first large-scale scene graph benchmark for sensitive content, comprising 13,999 frames from 219 movies annotated with Visual Genome-style scene graphs (25 object classes, 30 attributes including affective states such as pain, fear, aggression, and distress, 19 predicates) and 16 sensitivity tags across 5 categories. We distill a frontier VLM into a compact 241M student model using a multi-task recipe that addresses vocabulary imbalance in autoregressive scene graph generation through suffix-based object identity, Vocabulary-Aware Recall (VAR) Loss, and a decoupled Query2Label tag head with asymmetric loss, yielding a +6.4 percentage point improvement in SenBen Recall over standard cross-entropy training. On grounded scene graph metrics, our student model outperforms all evaluated VLMs except Gemini models and all commercial safety APIs, while achieving the highest object detection and captioning scores across all models, at 7.6× faster inference and 16× less GPU memory.

Vs frontier vision language models

SenBen test split, 2,000 frames

  • 🏆 Best Object Recall across 8 evaluated VLMs: 0.420 vs next-best Gemini 3 Pro 0.295

  • 🥈 Best SenBen-F1 among non-Gemini models: 0.428 vs Claude Opus 4.6 0.404 and GLM-4.6V 0.364

Vs content moderation APIs

SenBen test split, 16 MECD tag taxonomy

  • 🏆 Only model that covers all 16 MECD tags. Every commercial API and safety classifier covers at most 8.
  • 🏆 Highest tag F1: 0.594 (Q2L-bal) vs Azure Content Safety 0.430
  • 🏆 Highest safe / unsafe F1: 0.847 vs OpenAI Moderation 0.664
  • 🏆 Plus a full grounded scene graph (objects, attributes, predicates, bounding boxes) that no safety classifier produces

Efficiency

RTX 4090, fp32, beam search B=3

  • 🏆 733 ms / frame, lowest latency in our evaluation
  • 🏆 1.2 GB peak VRAM, 16 to 18× lower than open VLMs (18.8 GB Qwen3-VL, 21.5 GB GLM-4.6V)
  • 🏆 $0 per inference, vs $4.49 to $26.58 per 2K frames for proprietary APIs

Two operating points

Same architecture, different ASL gammas

  • Q2L-bal (γ⁻=4, γ⁺=1): tag-detection-focused. Highest tag F1 of our two models (0.594).
  • Q2L-agg (γ⁻=7, γ⁺=0): grounded-scene-graph-focused. Highest SenBen-F1 of our two models (0.431).
  • Pick whichever fits your moderation pipeline. Both 241M, both 733 ms, both $0 cost.

What is in this benchmark

  • 13,999 movie frames from 219 movies (1982 to 2023), split as 9,999 train / 2,000 val / 2,000 test.
  • Each frame is annotated with a Visual Genome aligned scene graph: 25 object classes, 30 attributes (including affective states pain, aggression, distress, pleasure, fear; body states naked, topless, bloody; etc.), and 19 predicates (stabbing, kissing, injecting, ...).
  • 16 MECD safety tags spanning 5 categories: immodesty, sexual, violence, substances, other.
  • The verbatim Gemini 3 Pro reasoning trace for each label, retained to support explainability research.
  • Bounding boxes are normalized to 0..1000 in [y_min, x_min, y_max, x_max] order, matching Visual Genome.
